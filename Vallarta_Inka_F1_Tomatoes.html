Attention Puerto Vallarta Tomato Growers!

Advanced virus-resistant Roma tomato seeds & transplants — now available locally

If you've tried growing tomatoes in Puerto Vallarta for any length of time, you've probably discovered the same frustrating reality: tomato viruses are one of the biggest challenges facing growers in our region.

Over the last two years, I have lost a significant portion of my tomato plants to viral diseases that are widespread throughout the Bay of Banderas area.

The Problem: Destructive Local Viruses

  • 🦠 Tomato Brown Rugose Fruit Virus (ToBRFV)
  • 🦠 Tomato Spotted Wilt Virus (TSWV)
  • 🦠 Tomato Mosaic Virus (ToMV)

For years, growers had very few options when these viruses appeared. However, beginning around 2023, new tomato hybrids were developed with genetic resistance specifically targeting these devastating diseases.

The challenge: These advanced commercial varieties have generally been available only to large-scale growers, typically sold in seed lots of 1,000 to 5,000 seeds — making them unavailable, impractical, and expensive for home gardeners and small market gardeners. And transplant-ready plants have never been available locally.

I have arranged to purchase a commercial quantity of one of these advanced hybrids and have received authorization from the supplier to offer my excess seeds locally to individuals and small growers.

Manufacturer's Description

Extremely vigorous and fast-growing plant with excellent foliage coverage, ideal for long production cycles. Strong root system with high resistance to nematodes. Short internodes with an average of 12 flowers and 10 fruits per cluster.

This hybrid is especially suited to hot climates, with genetic potential that allows it to thrive under high temperatures. Produces extra-large, firm fruit with exceptionally thick walls, intense red color, excellent flavor, and excellent shelf life.
